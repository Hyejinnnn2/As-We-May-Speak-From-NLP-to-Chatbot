Harry Potter and the Philosopher’s Stone 

Chapter 1 – The Boy Who Lived The story begins with the Dursley family
living ordinary lives on Privet Drive. Vernon Dursley notices strange
people in cloaks celebrating across the city. That night, Albus
Dumbledore meets Professor McGonagall and Hagrid. They bring baby Harry
Potter, the only survivor of an attack by Lord Voldemort. Harry is left
on the Dursleys’ doorstep with a letter explaining his tragic past.

Chapter 2 – The Vanishing Glass Ten years later, Harry grows up being
mistreated by the Dursleys while his cousin Dudley is spoiled. During a
visit to the zoo, Harry accidentally makes the glass of a snake
enclosure vanish. Strange incidents begin happening around him, though
he does not understand why.

Chapter 3 – The Letters from No One Harry receives mysterious letters
addressed to him at Privet Drive. Vernon panics and tries to avoid them
by moving the family across locations. The letters follow relentlessly.
Finally, they hide in a hut on a rocky island.

Chapter 4 – The Keeper of the Keys At midnight, Hagrid bursts in and
delivers Harry’s Hogwarts admission letter. He tells Harry about his
true identity as a wizard and explains the death of his parents at the
hands of Voldemort. Harry learns he is famous in the wizarding world.

Chapter 5 – Diagon Alley Hagrid takes Harry to London to buy school
supplies. Harry visits Gringotts Bank, meets goblins, buys his wand at
Ollivanders, and gets a glimpse of wizarding life. He also learns that
there is a mysterious item guarded in a high-security vault.

Chapter 6 – The Journey from Platform 9¾ Harry boards the Hogwarts
Express. He befriends Ron Weasley and meets Hermione Granger for the
first time. Draco Malfoy introduces himself arrogantly, but Harry
declines his friendship. Students arrive at Hogwarts.

Chapter 7 – The Sorting Hat During the Sorting Ceremony, the magical hat
considers sending Harry to Slytherin but ultimately places him in
Gryffindor. Ron and Hermione also join Gryffindor. The students settle
into castle life.

Chapter 8 – The Potions Master Harry attends his first classes and
experiences Snape’s hostility. Snape seems determined to humiliate him,
and Harry senses something suspicious about the professor’s behavior.

Chapter 9 – The Midnight Duel Draco Malfoy challenges Harry to a duel.
Although the duel is a trap, Harry, Ron, Hermione, and Neville stumble
upon a three-headed dog guarding a trapdoor. Hermione forces the boys to
avoid expulsion by becoming more cautious.

Chapter 10 – Halloween A troll enters Hogwarts. Hermione is attacked but
rescued by Harry and Ron. Their bravery solidifies their friendship.
Meanwhile, Harry notices Snape injured and limping.

Chapter 11 – Quidditch Harry plays his first Quidditch match. The broom
suddenly behaves violently as if cursed. Hermione sees Snape muttering
and assumes he is responsible. She sets fire to Snape’s robe to break
the curse.

Chapter 12 – The Mirror of Erised During Christmas break, Harry
discovers a magical mirror showing his deceased parents. Dumbledore
later explains the mirror shows “the deepest desire of one’s heart” and
warns Harry not to seek it again.

Chapter 13 – Nicolas Flamel Harry and friends discover the identity of
Nicolas Flamel, connected to the mysterious item in the vault: the
Philosopher’s Stone, an object that grants immortality. They suspect
someone at Hogwarts is trying to steal it.

Chapter 14 – Norbert the Norwegian Ridgeback Hagrid obtains a dragon egg
illegally. After it hatches, Harry and friends secretly send the dragon
to Ron’s brother Charlie. They get caught, earning heavy punishment.

Chapter 15 – The Forbidden Forest As detention, the group enters the
Forbidden Forest with Hagrid. Harry encounters a hooded creature
drinking unicorn blood, an act associated with Voldemort. Harry realizes
the Dark Lord may be regaining strength.

Chapter 16 – Through the Trapdoor Harry, Ron, and Hermione decide they
must protect the Philosopher’s Stone. Breaking school rules, they pass
through the trapdoor guarded by the three-headed dog. They overcome
magical challenges: Devil’s Snare, flying keys, a deadly chess match,
and a potion riddle. Harry proceeds alone.

Chapter 17 – The Man with Two Faces Harry discovers it is not Snape but
Professor Quirrell seeking the Stone under Voldemort’s influence. The
Mirror of Erised presents the Stone to Harry because he does not desire
to use it. Voldemort attempts to seize it, but Harry resists. Dumbledore
explains the Stone will be destroyed. Gryffindor wins the House Cup.
